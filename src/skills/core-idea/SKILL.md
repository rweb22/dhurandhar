# Core Idea

## Purpose

Every great endeavor begins with a single decision. Like when Yudhishthira decided to perform the Rajsuya sacrifice, this is where we crystallize your vision into one powerful statement.

**What you'll create:** A single, clear statement of what you're building and why.

## Before You Begin

You don't need:
- ❌ A detailed plan
- ❌ Technical specifications
- ❌ Complete requirements

You only need:
- ✅ A problem you want to solve
- ✅ An idea for a solution
- ✅ Willingness to question your assumptions

## Workflow

### Step 1: The Problem

**Question:** What problem are you solving?

Not what you want to build - what **problem** exists in the world that needs solving?

**Good examples:**
- "Developers waste hours searching for code examples across scattered documentation"
- "Small businesses can't afford enterprise inventory management software"
- "Remote teams struggle to maintain shared context during async work"

**Bad examples:**
- "I want to build a social network" (that's a solution, not a problem)
- "There should be an app for X" (too vague)

**Yudhishthira asks:** Why does this problem matter? Who suffers from it? What happens if it's not solved?

### Step 2: Who Experiences This Problem?

**Question:** Who specifically faces this problem?

Be precise. "Everyone" is not an answer. "Developers" is too broad.

**Good examples:**
- "Frontend developers working with legacy codebases"
- "Restaurant owners managing inventory in multiple locations"
- "Engineering teams of 5-20 people working across time zones"

**Bad examples:**
- "Users" (too vague)
- "People who need X" (circular definition)
- "Everyone in tech" (too broad)

**Yudhishthira asks:** Can you name three specific people who have this problem? What do they currently do to cope with it?

### Step 3: The Solution (One Sentence)

**Question:** What are you building to solve this problem?

One sentence. If you can't explain it in one sentence, you don't understand it yet.

**Format:** "A [type of product] that helps [specific users] [accomplish goal] by [key approach]"

**Good examples:**
- "A VS Code extension that helps frontend developers find React component examples by searching across GitHub in real-time"
- "A mobile app that helps restaurant owners track inventory across locations by scanning barcodes and auto-ordering supplies"
- "A Slack integration that helps remote engineering teams maintain context by automatically summarizing daily changes"

**Bad examples:**
- "A platform for developers" (too vague)
- "The Uber of X" (lazy analogy)
- "AI-powered solution for enterprise customers" (buzzword soup)

**Yudhishthira asks:** Can a stranger understand what you're building from this sentence? Could they repeat it back to you?

### Step 4: What Makes It Different?

**Question:** What's your unique approach?

Not "it's better" - **how** is it different from what exists?

**Good examples:**
- "Unlike Stack Overflow, we show code in context of full projects, not isolated snippets"
- "Unlike enterprise tools, we price per location ($20/month) not per user"
- "Unlike Loom, we create text summaries not video recordings"

**Bad examples:**
- "It's faster" (how?)
- "Better UX" (everyone says this)
- "AI-powered" (means nothing without specifics)

**Yudhishthira asks:** If this already existed, what would you be using? Why isn't that good enough?

### Step 5: The Constraint

**Question:** What's your biggest constraint?

Every project has limits. Name yours.

**Examples:**
- "Must work within browser - can't require installation"
- "Must cost less than $50/month for typical user"
- "Must integrate with existing Slack workspaces"
- "Solo founder - must ship MVP in 3 months"

**Yudhishthira asks:** What would make this impossible to build? What can you NOT compromise on?

### Step 6: Write the Core Idea

Now synthesize everything into a structured statement:

```markdown
# Core Idea

## Problem
[2-3 sentences describing the problem]

## Who
[1-2 sentences describing the specific users]

## Solution
[One sentence: "A [product] that helps [users] [goal] by [approach]"]

## Different Because
[2-3 sentences on unique approach]

## Key Constraint
[1 sentence on biggest limitation]

## Success Looks Like
[1-2 sentences - how do you know this worked?]
```
